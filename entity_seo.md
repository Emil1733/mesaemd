1. What is Entity SEO?
   Entity SEO is the process of building a website's "Authority Density" by referencing interconnected topics that Google’s algorithms already recognize as being related.

The Shift in Search: Ten years ago, Google lacked enough content and used keywords to find anything relevant. Today, Google has too much content and uses E-E-A-T (Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness) to find the best results.

The "Expert" Analogy: An expert on pests doesn't just know "ants"; they know ants, spiders, mice, and cockroaches. Entity SEO is about showing Google you know the entire "knowledge graph" of your industry.

Entity vs. Topical Authority: While similar, the speaker notes that Entity SEO is specifically high-leverage for local service providers, directories, and B2B businesses, whereas "Topical Authority" is often misapplied by general bloggers.

2. How Google Measures Authority
   Google doesn't "read" pages like a human; instead, it uses two primary signals to measure your entity relationship:

The Sitemap: The total list of pages you provide.

Keyword Density & Relationships: How closely related your pages are to one another.

The Scoring System: Topics closely related (e.g., "Pest Control" and "Bed Bugs") have a low relationship score (which is good). Topics far apart (e.g., "Pest Control" and "Real Estate") create a high score, which dilutes your authority.

3. Implementation Strategies
   For Local Services (e.g., Plumbers, Pest Control)
   Identify Entities: Use Google’s own search results. Search for "Common plumbing issues"—the list Google provides (leaky pipes, clogged toilets) represents the "entities" Google expects an authority to cover.

The Structure: Create individual, helpful service pages for every insect, rodent, or plumbing problem.

The Result: These pages collectively boost the authority of your Homepage, allowing it to rank for high-competition terms like "Pest Control Manhattan" even with a low Domain Rating (DR).

For B2B/SAS Companies
Feature & Use Case Pages: Instead of just one product page, create pages for specific "entities" like Inventory Tracking, Forecasting, or Warehouse Organization.

ICP Pages: Create pages tailored to different "Ideal Customer Profiles" or use cases to show expertise across the entire industry landscape.

For Location SEO
Neighborhood Entities: If targeting a major city (e.g., San Francisco), include pages or sections for specific neighborhoods (Sunset District, Richmond, etc.). Google recognizes these neighborhoods as entities belonging to the larger city entity.

4. The 3-Step Execution Checklist
   Step 1: Map the Connectivity
   Create an idea map of service pages, location pages, or use-case pages. Plan how they will link together via Global Navigation (Menus) and Footer Links to ensure Google’s crawlers see the connectivity.

Step 2: Design for Humans, Not Keywords
Zero Search Volume: Don't worry if a specific entity (like "cloth moths") has low search volume. Its presence on your site builds authority for the bigger keywords.

Avoid Fluff: Do not include "history of the city" or irrelevant filler. Focus on how you solve the specific problem related to that entity.

Step 3: Submit and Wait
Add all new pages to your XML sitemap. The speaker warns that Entity SEO is a slow game—expect to wait at least 3 months to see a significant lift in search visibility, as Google takes time to re-evaluate the cumulative score of a domain.
